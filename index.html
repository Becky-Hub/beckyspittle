About

UX Researcher | HCI PhD Candidate | Microsoft Research Fellow

I work at the intersection of research and interaction design, exploring how people perceive, navigate, and adapt to spatial multimodal interfaces.

I'm driven by a fascination with human behaviour, and how technology shapes (and is shaped by) everyday interactions. Through mixed-methods approaches, my goal is to bridge exploratory insights with product development to provide impactful user-centred solutions.

I was awarded the prestigious Microsoft Research PhD Fellowship, and have completed an internship with Tools for Thought at MSR Cambridge. Research contributions produced have been shared with wider groups at MSR, directly influencing product strategy.

I’m always up for meeting new people and learning something new! Outside of work I enjoy listening to music, playing video games and the acoustic guitar, reading witty non-fiction, travelling and looking at cute pictures of alpacas.
